On May 13, 2026 — five days ago — Rosario F. Perez, a Miami-Dade resident represented by Alvin Donald Scott Jr. (Florida Bar #110432, Consumer Protection & Insurance Attorneys), filed a putative TCPA class action in the Southern District of Florida (1:26-cv-23371) alleging violations of 47 U.S.C. § 227(b)(1)(A)(iii). The complaint pleads that Scentiment caused at least 56 unsolicited marketing text messages to be transmitted to the Subject Number between January 25, 2026 and April 27, 2026 — averaging more than four texts per week — and that the plaintiff had no prior business relationship with Scentiment, never provided her number, and never signed up for any communication. The complaint affirmatively alleges, on information and belief, that Scentiment obtained the plaintiff's number through the purchase of consumer data lists or other third-party data aggregation sources (paragraph 14). Damages: $500/violation negligent, $1,500/violation knowing/willful, class-wide; jury trial demanded.

The compliance gap is on the website, not just in the back-end. scentiment.com has no SMS Terms page — /policies/sms-terms, /pages/sms-terms, and /pages/mobile-terms all return 404. The Terms of Service tries a buried "purchase-equals-consent" theory in Section 13 — which is exactly the construct that fails the FCC's prior-express-written-consent standard for ATDS marketing. And the Privacy Policy openly admits acquiring contact data from "business partners, data providers, data licensors, public sources, and third-party platforms" — Perez's complaint paragraph 14 is functionally pre-conceded on the public-facing website. Product fit, in priority order: (1) Litigator Scrub, (2) Pre-Send List Hygiene & Federal/State DNC Scrubbing, (3) RND.

Pre-Send List Hygiene & Federal/State DNC Scrubbing

The current claim is § 227(b)(1)(A)(iii) — ATDS to cell, no consent. The Perez complaint explicitly notes the Subject Number was not on the Federal DNC Registry (paragraph 15) — meaning the ATDS theory stands independently. But the natural amended-complaint move is to add a § 227(c)(5) Federal DNC count against the broader class the moment one DNC-registered class member surfaces. Pre-send scrubbing against Federal DNC, state-level DNC, and your own internal DNC list is the cheapest single control that closes that door.  Product 3  ·  Close on this

Reassigned Number Database (RND) + Statutory Safe Harbor

Perez pleads she is "the regular and sole user" of the Subject Number — so this specific case is not a reassigned-number fact pattern. That's fine. The play is forward-looking. Smoking-Gun Compliance Gap

The wedge: Scentiment's own website pre-concedes paragraph 14 of the complaint

 Missing SMS Terms Page

No /sms-terms/ at any URL variant

Three standard SMS Terms URL variants tested — all return 404. No HELP/STOP keyword disclosure, no message frequency cap, no "msg & data rates may apply" notice, no "consent is not a condition of purchase" — anywhere on the site. The first thing plaintiff's counsel will do in discovery is screenshot the missing pages. Under FCC rules, SMS marketing requires conspicuous standalone disclosure of all of the above at the point of opt-in — none of which exists on this site.
 Privacy Policy Admits the Source Channel

"Business partners… data licensors… third-party platforms"

Verbatim from scentiment.com/policies/privacy-policy: "We may also collect information about you from: Business partners, such as advertising and joint marketing partners… Data providers, such as information services and data licensors… Public sources… Third-party platforms." The complaint's paragraph 14 — that Scentiment obtained the plaintiff's number "through the purchase of consumer data lists or through other third-party data aggregation sources" — is functionally pre-conceded on the defendant's own public-facing privacy disclosure.
 ToS "Purchase = Consent" Construct Fails FCC Standard

Buried Section 13 tries to bootstrap consent from purchase

Section 13 of the ToS: "By creating a User Account or purchasing one or more of our Products and providing us your contact information, you explicitly consent to receive marketing and other electronic communications…" This is precisely the construct that fails the FCC's prior-express-written-consent standard for ATDS marketing. Consent cannot be bundled into a purchase event, cannot be buried in ToS, and cannot be a condition of the transaction. The clause is the kind of language defense counsel will need to disavow, not lean on.
